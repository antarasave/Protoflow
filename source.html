Workflow

How it works

Four steps. Nothing to configure beyond your normal review habits.

  1. 01

    Describe your circuit

    Write what you needΓÇöblocks, interfaces, constraints. Plain language is fine.

  2. 02

    ProtoFlow generates a schematic

    First-pass symbols, nets, and sheets on disk. Expect to edit.

  3. 03

    Refine if needed

    Fix parts, run cleanup (Ctrl-U), check against the datasheet.

  4. 04

    Export to KiCad or Altium

    Hand off for layout, DRC, and release in the tool you already use.

ProtoFlow is not a PCB editor. It does not replace your layout toolchain.

FAQ

FAQ

Direct answers ΓÇö short, engineer-to-engineer. Update any product-specific limits as you ship.

Does ProtoFlow replace KiCad or Altium?

No. It targets schematic capture and prep, then export. Layout, DRC, manufacturing outputs, and org-wide library governance stay in your EDA toolchain.

Will the AI get my design ΓÇ£rightΓÇ¥ on the first try?

Assume no. Treat AI output as a first draft. You are responsible for datasheet-accurate pinouts, ratings, and design reviewΓÇösame as any generated code.

Where do my files live?

On your machine for the desktop workflow. If you add cloud features later, say exactly what leaves the device and when.

Do I need a credit card to download?

No for this landing pageΓÇÖs promiseΓÇöswap in your real billing policy if that changes.

What should I verify after export?

Symbol pin maps, power trees, default footprints, ERC in your EDA tool, and any analog-sensitive nets. Export is a handoff, not sign-off.
